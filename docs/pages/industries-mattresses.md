## Key Message

**"Complex product. Simple experience."**

Mattresses are one of the most technically complex products a consumer buys — and one of the most difficult to evaluate online. The result is high return rates and low buyer confidence. Imagine gives mattress brands the tools to make a complex product feel simple, visible, and trustworthy.

### 2. Mattress-Specific Challenges

**Heading:** Why mattresses are hard to sell online — and harder to sell well.

**Intro:** The online mattress purchase is an act of faith. Buyers are spending £600–£3,000 on a product they cannot try, whose internal construction is invisible, and whose comfort profile is subjective. Most brands respond with a comparison table and a free trial. Imagine goes further.

**Challenge cards (4):**

**Product explanation complexity**
A mattress with three firmness options, two height profiles, four comfort layer configurations, and five cover options is genuinely difficult to explain in copy and static imagery. Most buyers don't read the copy. They need to see the product.

**Returns from expectation mismatch**
Online mattress return rates run 10–20%. The primary driver is not product defect — it is buyer expectation mismatch. The mattress that arrived is not what the buyer thought they were buying. Visual configuration reduces this gap.

**Variant imagery gaps**
A mattress in four sizes and three cover options has twelve visual states. Most brands photograph the hero size in the hero cover and leave the rest to imagination. Buyers selecting a specific size or cover option see an image that doesn't match.

**Guided selling gap**
Mattress buying requires guidance — firmness, support type, sleeping position, temperature regulation. Brands that provide genuine guided selling convert at significantly higher rates. Most don't, because building a guided selling tool has historically required significant engineering investment.

**Design notes:** Four challenge cards in a 2x2 grid. Each card has a bold title, a short analytical paragraph, and a specific number or data point where relevant. Visuals should be restrained — the challenge section is text-led, not image-led.

---

### 3. How Imagine Solves It

**Heading:** Visualise the product. Guide the buyer. Reduce returns.

**Three solution pillars:**

**Visual product explanation**
Imagine renders mattress cross-sections with layer-by-layer accuracy — comfort foam, transition layer, support core, edge support, cover. Buyers can see what they're buying, not just read about it. Product comprehension improves. Returns fall.

**Real-time variant configuration**
Size, height, firmness, cover — every combination rendered and delivered to the PDP automatically. When a buyer selects King in the Linen cover, they see a King in a Linen cover — not the Queen in the Signature cover you had in stock for the photoshoot.

**Guided selling experience**
Imagine powers a guided selling flow that walks buyers through their requirements — sleeping position, firmness preference, partner considerations, temperature — and surfaces the right configuration with visual reinforcement at each step. The buyer arrives at the right product confident, not guessing.

**Design notes:** Three pillars in a horizontal layout. Each gets a visual: a cross-section render, a PDP showing variant selection with accurate imagery, and a guided selling UI flow. The guided selling visual is the most important — it should feel like a product demo in itself.

### 4. Guided Selling Feature Highlight

**Heading:** Turn "which one should I buy?" into a confident decision.

**Body:**
Most mattress buyers don't know their firmness preference, their support requirements, or which technology claims are meaningful to them. Guided selling that is genuinely visual — that shows buyers what changes as their answers change — converts browsers into buyers.

**Feature detail (3 items):**

1. **Adaptive questioning** — A short question flow that adapts based on previous answers. Couples see joint considerations. Side sleepers see shoulder pressure point information. The flow is smart, not scripted.

2. **Visual feedback at every step** — As the buyer answers questions, the recommended configuration updates — visually. They see the product changing in response to their needs. This builds trust in the recommendation.

3. **Clear recommendation with rationale** — The guided selling flow ends with a specific product recommendation, a visual of that configuration, and a plain-language explanation of why it's right for them. Buyers convert on confidence.

**Design notes:** Full-width section with a persistent guided selling UI mockup on the right side of the screen. Left side contains the feature list. This should feel like a product spotlight — the closest thing on this page to a live demo. Consider an animated or interactive version if technically feasible.
