# Page Spec: /careers

## Page Objective
Attract high-quality candidates — engineers, product people, and commercial operators — by being honest about what Imagine is building, where the company is, and what it is like to work there. The careers page should speak directly to people who make considered decisions about where they work.

## Audience
- Senior engineers and technical leads interested in hard infrastructure problems
- Product managers with experience in developer tools, commerce, or platform products
- Commercial operators (sales, partnerships, customer success) with B2B SaaS or ecommerce infrastructure backgrounds
- Designers interested in developer tooling and complex product configuration UI

## Key Message
Imagine is a small team working on a technically ambitious problem in a large and underdeveloped category. The work is hard in the right ways — real infrastructure, real customer problems, real engineering depth. If that is the kind of place you want to build, read on.

### 2. Why Imagine

**Heading:** Three reasons people join us.

**Reason 1 — The problem**
Heading: A real infrastructure problem.

Body: Most software companies are building better versions of things that already exist. Imagine is building infrastructure that does not exist at the level the problem deserves. Real-time rendering of physical products, constraint-based configuration logic, and a developer platform for a product category that has historically been served by point solutions stitched together with custom code. If you like hard problems with clear shape, this is one.

**Reason 2 — The product**
Heading: A platform you can be proud of.

Body: The Imagine API is designed with the same care we want in the products it powers. Clean endpoint naming, predictable error shapes, first-class TypeScript support, documentation that tells you what you actually need to know. We care about API quality the same way a designer cares about interface quality. The platform is what we are selling, and it shows.

**Reason 3 — The team**
Heading: A team that works well and ships.

Body: We are small enough that every person's judgment shapes the product and the company. We work asynchronously by default, communicate in writing, make decisions with clear reasoning, and hold ourselves to a high standard without creating a high-stress environment. We want people who do their best work when given the space to think and the trust to execute.

### 4. Culture Callout

**Heading:** How we work.

**Body (4 short points):**

**Remote and async by default**
We do not assume presence equals productivity. We write things down, make decisions in documents, and run meetings for decisions and connection — not status updates.

**Small team, large scope**
Everyone on the team owns a significant piece of something real. There are no hand-offs to teams of five. You will be the person who designs it, builds it, ships it, and learns from what happens.

**Direct feedback, no politics**
We give each other honest feedback and expect the same in return. We do not avoid difficult conversations. We also do not confuse directness with unkindness.

**Time to think**
We protect time for deep work. No meeting-heavy culture. No Slack always-on expectations. If the work requires sustained concentration — and most of our work does — you will have space for it.

## Copy Notes
- Write to the candidate, not about the company. "You will own..." not "The candidate will own..."
- The culture callout should be specific and honest, not a list of company virtues no one would dispute.
- Role descriptions should include enough context to self-qualify — a candidate should know from the description whether to apply.
- Avoid "we move fast", "startup environment", "wear many hats" — these are clichés that tell candidates nothing.

## Design Notes
- Hero: typographic, no photography required. Headcount and location details understated.
- Three-reason section: could be a 1-column editorial layout or a 3-column card layout
- Open roles: grouped by team, each role as an expandable card or linked detail page
- Culture callout: pull-quote style or short editorial section — not a bulleted list of perks
- Apply CTA: minimal — do not oversell. Candidates who have read the page will know if they want to apply.
