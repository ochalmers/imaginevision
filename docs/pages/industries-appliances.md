### 2. Appliance-Specific Challenges

**Heading:** The problems that are specific to your category.

**Intro:**
Appliance brands don't have a visualisation problem. They have a visualisation cost and speed problem. The solution they've been using — photoshoots — doesn't scale with modern colour ranges, and the operational cost of distributing assets to retail partners compounds on top.

**Challenge cards (4):**

**Colour variant photography cost**
A premium appliance brand launching 12 new colourways across a range of 8 products needs 96 new hero images — at a minimum. At £300–£600 per product image, that's £30,000–£58,000 before lifestyle shots, retailer crops, and platform resizes. This is not a marketing cost. It is a structural constraint on how fast you can launch.

**Colourway launch complexity**
Getting a new colour from design approval to live on retail partner sites currently takes 6–10 weeks. By the time assets reach Currys, John Lewis, and your DTC store, the window for a colour trend has often passed. Speed to market is a competitive differentiator that most appliance brands are losing.

**Retail partner asset management**
Each retail partner has different asset requirements — image sizes, naming conventions, background treatments, lifestyle ratios. Managing this across 20–50 partners for a full colour range is an operational burden that sits on someone's desk, perpetually behind.

**Consistency across channels**
When imagery is produced across multiple photoshoot cycles, consistency degrades. The Sage Green on your DTC site looks different from the Sage Green on the retailer's site because they were shot six months apart under different lighting. Imagine renders from the same source file every time — consistency is structural, not aspirational.

**Design notes:** Four cards, 2x2 grid. Each card has a bold title and a short, analytically specific paragraph. Real numbers where possible. Avoid genericism — "photography is expensive" is not the same as "£30,000–£58,000 for a colourway launch."

### 3. How Imagine Solves It

**Heading:** Render once. Distribute everywhere.

**Three solution pillars:**

**Instant colour variant rendering**
Upload your 3D appliance models. Define your colour and finish library. Imagine generates every colourway combination — hero angles, lifestyle context, retailer-specific crops — without a photoshoot. A new colour is a render parameter, not a production booking. Time from colour approval to live imagery: 48–72 hours.

**New colourway launch pipeline**
When you approve a new colourway, Imagine generates the full asset set automatically — every product, every angle, every format, every channel. Your DTC store, your retail partner feeds, and your trade catalogue are updated simultaneously. The launch is a pipeline run.

**Automated retail partner asset distribution**
Imagine maintains retailer asset specifications for each of your distribution partners. When new imagery is generated, it is formatted, named, and delivered to each partner according to their requirements — automatically. The spreadsheet, the FTP upload, and the "can you resend in the right format" email disappear.

**Design notes:** Three pillars in a structured layout. Each pillar has a headline, a short paragraph, and a specific time or cost benefit statement in larger type. Visual for each: a colour variant render grid, a pipeline schematic, a retailer asset distribution dashboard.
