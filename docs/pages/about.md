# Page Spec: /about

## Page Objective
Tell the Imagine story — why the company exists, what it is building, and what it values — in a way that builds trust with prospective customers, prospective employees, and investors. The about page should feel written, not assembled from a brand brief.

## Audience
- Prospective customers doing due diligence on Imagine as a platform and partner
- Prospective employees evaluating whether Imagine is a company worth building with
- Investors and analysts building context on the company and its founders
- Press and analysts covering the configurable commerce space

## Key Message
Imagine was started because the infrastructure for selling configurable physical products online did not exist at the quality level the problem deserved. We are building it: the API, the rendering engine, the configuration logic, and the commerce primitives that physical product brands and their developers need.

### 2. Origin Story

**Heading:** Where we started.

**Body (3–4 paragraphs):**

The founders of Imagine spent time inside brands selling configurable physical products — furniture, lighting, kitchen equipment — and kept encountering the same problem. The products were sophisticated and well-designed. The online experience for configuring and purchasing them was not. Customers were being asked to make high-stakes decisions — committing to a sofa in a specific fabric at a specific size, delivered in 8 weeks — based on a photograph that may not have shown the fabric they selected, in a room that looked nothing like theirs.

The tooling available to brands was either built for the wrong problem (generic ecommerce platforms not designed for physical product configuration) or required enormous custom engineering investment for every new brand that attempted it. The problem was being solved repeatedly, poorly, at great cost.

The insight was that this is an infrastructure problem. The configuration logic, the rendering pipeline, the order payload construction, the pricing modifier handling — these are solved problems if you build them once, properly, as a platform. Every brand that comes after does not need to solve them again.

Imagine was founded to build that platform. The API came first, because we believe that the right abstraction layer is the one that gives developers full control. The SDK, the components, and the rendering engine followed — each one reducing the distance between a brand's product catalog and a customer who can confidently configure and buy.

---

### 3. What We're Building

**Heading:** What Imagine is.

**Body (structured, 4 points):**

**A rendering engine for physical products**
Photorealistic, real-time rendering of configurable products in contextual scenes. Built to handle the visual complexity of materials, finishes, light, and scale — not just colour swaps on a white background.

**A configuration engine for complex option logic**
The rules that govern what configurations are valid — fabric and leg compatibility, dimension constraints, material exclusions — modelled as a proper constraint system, not a spreadsheet maintained in a CMS.

**An API and SDK for developers**
The entire platform is accessible through a clean REST API and a fully-typed JavaScript SDK. Developers build the experience. Imagine handles the infrastructure underneath.

**Infrastructure for commerce, not a storefront**
Imagine does not replace your ecommerce platform. It adds the configurable product layer your platform was never designed to handle — and exposes it through APIs that connect to any commerce stack.

### 5. Values

**Heading:** What we believe.

Four values, each with a title and a 2–3 sentence description. Written to be specific, not generic.

**Precision over simplification**
Physical products are genuinely complex. We do not paper over that complexity with clever UX — we model it accurately and give developers the tools to make it legible. A configuration engine that gets the rules wrong is worse than not having one.

**Developer trust is earned through quality**
We are building infrastructure for developers who have a choice. They will evaluate our API, our SDK, our documentation, and our reliability — and they will build on what they trust. We compete on quality of execution, not on marketing.

**Honesty about what we are and are not**
Imagine is infrastructure. We are not a storefront, a CMS, or a design tool. Being clear about the boundary of what we do makes us a better partner to the platforms and agencies we work alongside.

**Ship with intent**
We ship when things are ready, not when a date is on a calendar. Every change to the platform is a commitment to the customers running integrations on top of it. We take that seriously.

## Copy Notes
- The origin story should read like something a founder wrote, not a PR agency.
- Avoid superlatives ("world-class", "best-in-class", "pioneering"). The work should make those claims unnecessary.
- Values should be specific enough that a candidate could evaluate whether they agree. Generic values (integrity, innovation) tell you nothing.
- Team section: even with placeholder names, the roles should reflect actual org structure.

## Design Notes
- Hero: typographic, no imagery. The mission statement should carry the weight.
- Origin story: wide column, generous line-height, editorial reading experience
- Values: displayed as a 2x2 grid or single column depending on copy length
- Team section: photo grid with name and role, links to LinkedIn optional
- Investor section: logo row, understated
- Page should feel considered and unhurried — not a sales page
