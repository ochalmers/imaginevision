### 2. What a Configurator Looks Like

**Heading:** Everything your buyers need to decide with confidence.

**Intro:** A great configurator isn't just a set of swatches. It's a real-time visual conversation between your product and your buyer.

**Feature list (6 items, icon + label + one-line description):**

1. **Real-time 3D rendering** — Every configuration renders instantly. No loading spinners, no pre-baked image grids.
2. **Valid state enforcement** — Configuration logic prevents buyers from selecting impossible combinations. No surprises at checkout.
3. **Multi-angle views** — Buyers can orbit, zoom, and examine their configuration from every angle.
4. **Material and finish accuracy** — Fabrics, leathers, powdercoats, and laminates rendered with photorealistic fidelity.
5. **Contextual placement** — Room scenes and lifestyle context automatically updated to reflect the chosen configuration.
6. **Checkout integration** — The exact configuration — SKU, materials, dimensions — passes cleanly to your cart and OMS.

**Design notes:** Feature list as two-column grid. Icon-driven. Keep descriptions to one sentence each. Consider a persistent product render on the right side of this section that updates as you scroll past each feature.

---

### 3. How It Works

**Heading:** Built on Imagine's configuration engine — not custom code.

**Steps (numbered, horizontal flow on desktop):**

1. **Ingest your product catalogue** — Upload your 3D assets, materials library, and configuration rules via the Imagine API or our onboarding workflow.
2. **Define your configuration logic** — Set valid states, conditional rules, and pricing logic. Our schema handles complexity your catalogue actually has.
3. **Embed the configurator** — Drop our SDK into your storefront — Shopify, custom, headless. Styling is yours to control.
4. **Connect to checkout** — Configuration output maps to your existing cart, OMS, and fulfilment workflow with no custom middleware required.

**Design notes:** Horizontal step flow with connective lines. Clean numbering. Consider a code snippet showing the embed — even one line of SDK usage builds developer confidence and signals simplicity.
