### 2. Furniture-Specific Challenges

**Heading:** The complexity your category actually has.

**Intro:** Furniture sits at the intersection of high configuration complexity, expensive production, and long purchase consideration. The problems stack.

**Challenge cards (4):**

**Variant explosion**
A mid-size furniture brand offering a sofa in 80 fabrics, 6 leg finishes, and 4 configurations has over 1,900 SKU combinations. Photographing even 10% of those requires a standing studio operation. Most brands photograph 2–5 hero options and leave the rest invisible.

**Lifestyle photography cost**
A full lifestyle shoot for a single furniture hero — art direction, studio hire, props, photographer, post-production — costs £8,000–£25,000. Running that for every new season, every new colourway, and every new range is not sustainable.

**B2B catalogue management**
Wholesale buyers — interior designers, retailers, contract furniture buyers — need accurate, up-to-date product imagery for their own quotes and presentations. Most brands deliver a PDF catalogue that is out of date before it's printed.

**COM and bespoke configuration**
Higher-end furniture brands offer COM — buyers bring their own fabric. Visualising a custom configuration before production requires a physical sample or an educated guess. Returns and remakes follow.

**Design notes:** Four cards in a 2x2 grid. Each card: bold challenge title, short descriptive paragraph with real specifics. No stock photography — use a visual that reflects the challenge (e.g., a product page with a missing image, a stack of printed catalogues).

### 3. How Imagine Solves It

**Heading:** One platform, three problems solved.

**Three solution pillars:**

**Automated variant imagery**
Upload your 3D furniture models and materials library. Imagine generates product imagery for every fabric and finish combination — hero angles, lifestyle context, swatch crops — without photography. When you add a new fabric grade, the imagery generates automatically. Your product pages stay complete.

**DTC configurator**
Your buyers can configure their piece — frame, fabric, leg finish, dimensions — and see it rendered in real time before they add to cart. Valid state logic prevents impossible combinations. The exact configuration passes to your checkout and OMS. Average conversion lift across furniture customers: 34%.

**Wholesale catalogue automation**
Imagine generates and maintains a live digital catalogue for your wholesale partners — always current, always accurate. Partners can access imagery, configure products, and download assets for their own sales materials. The PDF catalogue problem disappears.

**Design notes:** Three-column layout or tabbed section. Each pillar gets a headline, a short paragraph, and a relevant product visual. The visual should show the output — a live configurator, a rendered variant grid, a wholesale portal.
