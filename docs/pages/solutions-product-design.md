## Key Message

**"Design, visualise, iterate — without the studio."**

Physical samples cost thousands and take weeks. Imagine lets design teams see, test, and validate products before a single sample is made — compressing development timelines and reducing the cost of iteration.

### 2. The Workflow

**Heading:** From design file to photorealistic render — in the same week.

**Intro:** Imagine plugs into your existing design workflow. You don't rebuild your process around it. You add a visualisation layer that makes every stage of development faster.

**Four workflow stages (horizontal):**

1. **Design**
Work in your existing tools — CAD, 3D modelling software, whatever your team uses. Imagine accepts standard 3D formats. You don't need to rebuild assets for our platform.

2. **Visualise**
Upload your model and materials library. Imagine generates photorealistic renders — product on white, in context, with accurate material fidelity. See how a powder-coat finish looks under natural light. See how a fabric drapes. See the product as a buyer would.

3. **Iterate**
Change a finish, swap a material, adjust a proportion. Renders update without a new sample. Your design team can explore ten directions in the time it would have taken to make one prototype.

4. **Launch**
The same assets used for design validation become the product imagery on your PDP, in your marketing, and in your sales materials. No reshoots at launch. The work is already done.

**Design notes:** Four stages presented as a horizontal timeline with a persistent product render that visually evolves across each stage. Stage 3 (iterate) should show multiple variants side by side — this is the moment the value is clearest.

---

### 3. What You Can See and Test

**Heading:** The fidelity your design decisions require.

**Feature grid (6 items):**

1. **Material accuracy** — Fabrics, leathers, veneers, laminates, powdercoats, and lacquers rendered with accurate light response and texture fidelity. Suitable for design sign-off, not just reference.

2. **Finish and colourway testing** — Test every finish combination before committing to physical development. See which colourways work and which don't — without making samples of each.

3. **Proportional validation** — Place products in scaled environments to validate proportions. Understand how a piece reads in a room before it's built.

4. **Construction detail** — Joinery details, hardware finishes, upholstery seams — rendered at a level of detail that supports design review and approval.

5. **Lighting conditions** — Test how a product reads under different lighting conditions — natural, retail, residential. Critical for finishes that behave differently in different environments.

6. **Contextual placement** — See the product in the room context it's designed for. How does the sofa read in a small living room versus a large open plan? Validate before the sample exists.

**Design notes:** Two-column grid. Icon + label + two-sentence description. Keep it clean and precise — this is a design audience. Avoid hyperbole. Let the feature descriptions do the work.
