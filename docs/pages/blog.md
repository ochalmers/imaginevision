# Page Spec: /blog

## Page Objective
Establish Imagine as the clearest, most credible voice on configurable commerce — the category Imagine is building. The blog is a long-term compounding asset for category authority, organic discovery, and trust with both commercial and technical audiences.

## Audience
- Ecommerce and digital product leads at furniture, mattress, appliance, and lighting brands
- Technical leads and developers interested in commerce infrastructure
- Strategy, product, and category thinkers interested in the future of physical product retail
- Press, analysts, and investors building a picture of the configurable commerce space

## Key Message
The people building the infrastructure for configurable commerce have something worth saying about it. Imagine's blog publishes thinking that is useful whether or not you are a customer.

## Copy Notes
- Post titles should be specific and intellectually honest — avoid vague thought leadership titles.
- Author bylines matter: name and role signal credibility. Founders and senior team should write here.
- Category tags should be mutually exclusive and stable — resist adding new categories for single posts.
- Frequency note in the hero and subscribe section: "when we have something worth saying" — this is a quality signal, not an excuse.
- The blog should not recycle press releases or product announcements. Those belong in /product-updates.

## Design Notes
- Featured post at full width signals editorial curation — it should change with each new post
- Post cards: minimal — no hero images required. Title, category, author, date is sufficient.
- Category browsing section can be a horizontal scroll on mobile
- Subscribe form embedded inline — not in a modal or overlay
- Author photos optional but recommended for founder/leadership posts
