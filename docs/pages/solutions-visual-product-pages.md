# Page Spec: /solutions/visual-product-pages

## Meta
- **URL:** /solutions/visual-product-pages
- **Title:** Visual Product Pages — Imagine
- **Description:** Imagine automatically generates product imagery for every variant at scale — replacing manual photography with rendered images that update the moment your catalogue does.
- **Audience:** Heads of ecommerce, digital merchandising leads, creative operations managers at brands with large or growing variant counts
- **Page objective:** Make the cost and speed case for automated variant imagery versus photography. Show that Imagine produces output that is production-ready, not a compromise.

---

## Key Message

**"Every variant. Every angle. Every page."**

Most brands photograph their hero SKU and leave the rest. Buyers land on product pages with no image for the colour they want, and they leave. Imagine fixes this — by generating accurate, photorealistic imagery for every configuration, automatically.

---

## Tone & Design Direction

- Lead with the output. Show rendered variant images alongside each section.
- The problem section should feel genuinely painful — real numbers, real friction.
- Solution section is clean and confident — not overselling, just demonstrating.
- Apple-adjacent: let the product images do the talking.

### 2. The Problem

**Heading:** Photo production doesn't scale with your catalogue.

**Body:**
Every new colourway needs a photographer, a studio, a sample, a stylist, and weeks of lead time. Meanwhile, buyers land on your product page and see a grey placeholder where the navy finish should be.

**Problem callouts (3, horizontal):**

1. **Cost per variant** — Professional product photography runs £300–£800 per SKU. A 200-variant catalogue costs six figures before post-production.
2. **Time to publish** — Average time from new variant decision to live imagery: 6–10 weeks. By then, the launch window has passed.
3. **Coverage gaps** — Most brands visualise fewer than 40% of their active variants. The rest ship without imagery — or with a swatch chip and a description.

**Design notes:** Three callout cards, icon + stat + short description. Consider a visual showing a product page with a missing image placeholder on one side, versus a fully visualised page on the other.

---

### 3. The Solution

**Heading:** Generate once. Every variant, covered.

**Body:**
You provide the 3D asset and materials library. Imagine generates every combination — every fabric, finish, colour, and configuration — as production-ready rendered images. Delivered to your PDP automatically.

**How it works (3 steps):**

1. **Ingest your product and materials** — Upload your 3D model and materials library. Imagine maps every valid configuration.
2. **Render at scale** — Our rendering pipeline generates every variant combination — multiple angles, lifestyle context, swatch crops — without manual intervention.
3. **Deliver to your storefront** — Images are delivered via API or direct integration to your ecommerce platform. Variant selection on the PDP automatically surfaces the right image.

**Design notes:** Step flow with a product render appearing on each step — showing the input (3D model), the pipeline (render grid), and the output (live PDP). Keep it visual and clean.

---

### 4. Output Types

**Heading:** Every image your PDP needs.

**Output grid (4 items):**

1. **Hero renders** — Clean product-on-white imagery at every angle, for every variant. Ready for your primary PDP image slot.
2. **Lifestyle context shots** — Product placed in room scenes that reflect the chosen finish and material — automatically selected or generated.
3. **Swatch crops** — Tight, accurate material crops for colour/fabric selectors. Consistent across the catalogue.
4. **Zoom-ready detail shots** — High-resolution material and construction details for buyers who inspect before they buy.

**Design notes:** 2x2 image grid showing an example of each output type. Real renders — not mockups. This section must feel like proof, not promise.
