### 2. The Problem: Lighting Is Hard to Sell Online

**Heading:** The challenges lighting brands know well.

**Body intro:** Lighting products are visually complex. They have a wide range of form factors, an expanding set of finish options, and a customer who needs to see the product in context — not on a white background — to make a confident purchase decision. The current toolkit is not built for this.

**Challenge 1 — Context photography is expensive and unscalable**
A pendant in brushed brass looks completely different in a kitchen versus a dining room, at a 3m ceiling versus a 5m atrium. Traditional photography cannot cover every fixture, every finish, every room type. Brands end up with a handful of hero shots and a customer left guessing.

**Challenge 2 — Finish variants multiply the visual burden**
A single fixture offered in 8 finishes with 3 room contexts is 24 images. Add cable length and shade options, and the matrix expands rapidly. Most brands photograph 1–2 variants and write copy for the rest. That gap costs conversion.

**Challenge 3 — The trade channel needs specification tools, not just product pages**
Architects and interior designers specify lighting early — often before a final finish decision is made. They need a tool that lets them see a fixture in a room, change the finish, and generate a specification document. An ecommerce PDP does not serve this workflow.

**Challenge 4 — Warmth and colour temperature are invisible in static images**
2700K warm white and 4000K cool white are meaningfully different in a residential context. Static photography captures one. Buyers make the wrong choice, returns increase.

### 3. The Solution: Infrastructure for Lighting Brands

**Heading:** Built for the visual complexity of light.

**Solution 1 — In-room contextual rendering**
Imagine's rendering engine places your fixtures into photorealistic room contexts — kitchens, dining rooms, hotel lobbies, retail spaces, outdoor terraces. Every render is physically accurate: shadow direction, light cast, ceiling height all respond to the fixture's geometry and light source properties.

Every room context is available for every product. No separate photoshoot per room type.

**Solution 2 — Finish and material variants at scale**
Define your finish library once — brushed brass, matte black, polished nickel, antique bronze, powder-coat white. Imagine renders every product in every finish programmatically. Launch a new finish across your entire catalog in hours, not months.

**Solution 3 — Colour temperature visualisation**
Imagine renders the light cast by your fixture, not just the fixture itself. Toggle between 2700K, 3000K, and 4000K to show customers how the light transforms a room. A feature that static photography cannot replicate.

**Solution 4 — Specification sheet generation**
The Imagine API can generate trade-ready specification documents from any saved configuration — including the rendered context image, product dimensions, finish details, and electrical specification. Serve the architect channel without a separate workflow.

## Copy Notes
- Lead with the customer problem, not the product feature. Lighting buyers know their pain.
- Colour temperature rendering is a genuine differentiator — call it out specifically.
- Trade/specification tool angle is often undercommunicated in lighting sales — make it prominent.
- Avoid generic ecommerce language (AOV, conversion rate) in the hero. Use language lighting brands use: specification, finish, context, warmth.

## Design Notes
- Full-bleed renders should be hero-sized and high quality
- Finish selector in the hero should be interactive where possible
- Problem section: understated, editorial tone — not alarmist
- Solution section: paired with supporting visuals for each solution point
- Room context highlight should show scale comparison clearly
